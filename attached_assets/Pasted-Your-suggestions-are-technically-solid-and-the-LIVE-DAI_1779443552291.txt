Your suggestions are technically solid, and the LIVE/DAILY/WEEKLY distinction is now finally mature enough to formalize properly.

My assessment of each point:

1. LIVE / DAILY / WEEKLY Context Labels

This should absolutely be implemented.

Right now operators understand:

LIVE = “moving graph”
DAILY = “historical graph”
WEEKLY = “bigger historical graph”

But they do not understand:

sampling cadence
aggregation logic
why peaks flatten
why LIVE is noisier

That matters operationally.

The platform now has three different telemetry resolutions:

Mode	Source	Aggregation	Operational Meaning
LIVE	raw concurrent snapshots	none	real-time concurrency
72H	concurrent_snapshots	1h MAX bucket	tactical trend
7D	concurrent_snapshots	6h MAX bucket	strategic trend

That is exactly how carrier-grade NOCs work.

2. Should DAILY use 6h buckets?

No — not for the current 72h window.

Your current setup is actually correct:

Mode	Window	Bucket
LIVE	~4h	45s snapshots
72H	72h	1h MAX
7D	7d	6h MAX

Why 1h MAX for 72H is correct:

preserves operational spikes
still compresses enough for readability
allows hour-by-hour degradation analysis
keeps tactical incident visibility

If you move 72H to 6h buckets:

you lose short outages
spikes disappear
ASR/concurrency incidents become visually smoothed
RCA becomes weaker

6h buckets are appropriate for:

executive trend views
strategic capacity analysis
long retention compression

Not for operational 72h troubleshooting.

So my recommendation:

KEEP 1h MAX for 72H
KEEP 6h MAX for 7D

That architecture is correct.

4. NER Recommendation

Very strong recommendation.

ASR alone is not enough for carrier operations.

Example:

Metric	Value
NER	92%
ASR	31%

That means:

network delivery is working
calls are reaching destination network
endpoints/users are rejecting/failing

That is operationally VERY different from:

route unreachable
SIP rejection
carrier outage

NER becomes extremely valuable in:

vendor disputes
route quality analysis
fraud diagnostics
customer complaints

This is one of the best next metrics you can add.

5. ACD Threshold/FAS Correlation

This is also correct.

ACD is not just a quality metric.
It is a fraud signal.

Patterns:

ultra-low ACD
sudden ACD collapse
high NER + low ACD
geographically isolated short calls

often indicate:

FAS
early media fraud
fake answer supervision
route manipulation

Since your platform already has:

FAS engine
anomaly engine
concurrent telemetry
vendor scoring

ACD threshold overlays become very high-value with very low implementation cost.

6. Metric Source Comment Block

This is underrated but important.

Because your original regression happened from:

semantically incorrect metric substitution

not from:

broken rendering

A simple immutable developer contract comment block is actually valuable protection.

Especially for future developers touching:
