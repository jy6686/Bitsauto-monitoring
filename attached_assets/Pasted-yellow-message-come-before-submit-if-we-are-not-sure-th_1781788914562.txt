Your question is exactly the right one:

"If BitsAuto can fetch rates from Sippy, how can it not have permission to modify rates?"

Because read permissions and write permissions are separate in Sippy.

Your screenshot actually proves this.

What the screenshot proves

You are logged in as:

ssp-root

You can open:

/c1/rates_tariff.php?i_tariff=33

You can see:

Download Rates ✅
Upload Rates button ✅
Add Rate button ✅
Delete Rate button ✅

So from a human browser perspective, ssp-root appears to have access to the rate editor page.

That is why I do not fully trust the yellow warning message.

Why the warning is suspicious

The yellow box says:

Rate push will fail
Sippy permission required
ssp-root lacks Edit Tariff Rates permission

But your Sippy screenshot shows:

Upload Rates
Add Rate
Delete All
Delete Row

If the account truly lacked rate-edit permission, usually Sippy would:

hide Upload button
hide Add button
hide Delete buttons

or redirect away.

Instead, the page is fully editable.

That means one of three things is happening:

Scenario 1 (most likely)

BitsAuto is checking the wrong page and assuming failure.

Example:

/admin/tariffs.php

returns:

read-only

BitsAuto shows warning.

But actual upload page:

/c1/rates_tariff.php

is writable.

This is very possible.

Scenario 2

Upload succeeds but POST format is wrong.

Meaning:

GET /c1/rates_tariff.php

works

but

POST upload

fails because:

wrong field name
wrong hidden token
wrong form action
wrong XLSX structure

In this case the permission warning is misleading.

Scenario 3

RTS1 account configured in Settings is different from browser account.

The warning text mentions:

configured Rate Admin account

If BitsAuto uses:

RTS1

for uploads

while browser uses:

ssp-root

then:

ssp-root can edit manually
RTS1 cannot

This would explain everything.

My conclusion

Based on your Sippy screenshot, I am not convinced this is a permission problem.

The evidence currently suggests:

BitsAuto can authenticate.
BitsAuto can read tariffs.
ssp-root can open the upload page.
Upload controls are visible.
Legacy BitsAuto performed the same workflow successfully.

So the more likely issue is:

Wrong upload POST format,
Wrong form field names,
Missing hidden token,
Wrong upload action URL,
Or BitsAuto is using a different credential (RTS1) than the browser session.

I would stop treating this as a permission issue until the deployment logs prove that the upload POST is actually reaching /c1/rates_tariff.php and receiving a rejection from Sippy. Right now the screenshots suggest the warning may simply be wrong.
