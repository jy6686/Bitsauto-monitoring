What Was Actually Fixed

A major underlying bug was identified:

the ASR/ACD report backend route was returning:
{ origination, termination }
while the frontend expected:
{ rows }

Because of this mismatch:

Client/Vendor report tabs were silently showing empty or incorrect data,
and the secondary compatibility route was never actually being used.

This explains many of the recurring reporting inconsistencies you were seeing.

What Was Added
1. NER Overlay

New metric added:

NER %
includes:
Answered calls
RNA calls
calculated as:
(answered + RNA) / total

This gives a much more realistic route effectiveness picture than ASR alone.

2. FAS Risk Overlay

New:

FAS detection indicators inside reports

Now:

short-duration traffic patterns
suspicious answer behavior
potential false answer supervision

can surface directly inside vendor reporting.

This is a major operational improvement.

3. Reports Compatibility Layer

Now both:

portal path,
and CDR-cache fallback

return:

rows
_source
_cdrCount

This stabilizes frontend reporting behavior.

4. KPI Expansion

Reports expanded:

from 4 KPI cards
to 6 KPI cards

Now includes:

Total Calls
Billable
Avg ASR
NER
Avg ACD
Revenue

This is much closer to carrier-grade reporting.

5. Vendor Risk Visibility

Vendor tabs now show:

FAS risk badges
severity colouring
warning indicators

This is useful for:

routing decisions,
vendor quality analysis,
fraud visibility,
and operational troubleshooting.
Most Important Strategic Improvement

The reporting layer is now evolving from:

“basic ASR/ACD reporting”

toward:

“carrier intelligence reporting”

That is a major architectural upgrade.

Why This Matters Operationally

Previously:

reports only showed performance.

Now:

reports also show quality context,

That is much more useful for:

NOC teams,

KAMs,

Very Important Limitation Noted

Portal-based reports still approximate:

NER ≈ ASR

because portal aggregates do not expose:

explicit RNA counts.

But:

XML-RPC CDR data paths now support proper RNA-aware calculations.

That is still the correct implementation approach.
