Your suggestion to add NER to the dashboard is strong and operationally valuable, especially for telecom and VoIP monitoring. Based on the current codebase and available Sippy CDR data, here’s what makes the most sense conceptually before implementation:

Recommended Direction

The best approach would be to show ASR and NER together in the same dashboard card instead of creating a completely separate standalone KPI.

This gives immediate operational insight:

High NER + Low ASR → network is healthy, users are not answering
Low NER + Low ASR → routing/network/carrier issue
High ASR + High NER → healthy traffic

This becomes much more actionable for:

NOC teams
Carrier management
Routing analysis
Vendor quality comparison
Suggested Dashboard Layout

Current:

Active Calls
ASR
Traffic Score
MOS

Suggested:

Active Calls
ASR / NER Combined
Traffic Score
MOS
PDD (optional)
Suggested ASR / NER Card Example
Writing

Network Effectiveness

NER: 94%
ASR: 61%
Gap: 33%

Status:
Network delivery is healthy.
Low ASR is likely caused by user behavior (busy / no answer / rejected calls).

Why This Is Better Than Only ASR

ASR alone can be misleading.

Example:

Carrier A → ASR 45%
Carrier B → ASR 45%

Without NER:
Both look equally bad.

With NER:

Carrier A → NER 91%
Carrier B → NER 48%

Now you know:

Carrier A is actually healthy
Carrier B has serious routing/network problems
Strong Suggestion: Add “Estimated NER”

Since Sippy maps SIP codes internally and not all SIP causes are directly exposed, the dashboard should display:

“Estimated NER”
or
“eNER”

This keeps the reporting transparent and technically accurate.

Suggested Future Enhancements (Do Not Implement Yet)
1. Vendor-wise NER

Very powerful for:

carrier comparison

2. NER Trend Graph

Compare:

ASR vs NER over time
day/night patterns

This becomes extremely valuable for call center traffic analysis.

3. Use NER in Traffic Score Formula

Current formula heavily relies on ASR.

A better quality formula would weight:

NER (network quality)
MOS (media quality)
PDD (signaling speed)
ASR (commercial success)

This separates:

network problems
from
customer behavior

much more accurately.

Important Technical Suggestion

Do not fabricate NER using random logic or guessed values.

The current approach using:

connected calls
subscriber rejection
no answer
switched off

is acceptable as a telecom-grade estimated NER model if clearly labeled.

That is far better than artificial/random MOS-style calculations.

My Overall Recommendation
Keep one canonical MOS engine
Add Estimated NER beside ASR
Use NER for network-quality evaluation
Keep ASR for business/commercial visibility
Avoid random KPI generation completely

This would move the dashboard much closer to a real carrier-grade telecom monitoring platform rather than a generic VoIP statistics panel.
