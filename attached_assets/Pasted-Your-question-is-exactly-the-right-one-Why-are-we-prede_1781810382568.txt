Your question is exactly the right one:

Why are we predefining rates?

We should not be inventing or predefining any tariff data.

The safest workflow is:

Read tariff from Sippy
↓
Get all existing rows
↓
Get existing IDs
↓
Get existing Country
↓
Get existing Intervals
↓
Get existing Grace Period
↓
Get existing Activation Date
↓
Modify ONLY requested destination(s)
↓
Upload complete tariff back

Nothing else should be generated.

What concerns me

The log proposal says:

Full Tariff Upload Preview
Action=U ID=46 Prefix=1880 Rate=0.009
...

That is fine only if those values came from Sippy's downloaded tariff.

If the code contains:

rate = 0.009
interval1 = 1
intervalN = 1
grace = 1

hardcoded anywhere,

then that is dangerous.

The upload builder should never contain tariff defaults.

Instead:

existingRate.price1
existingRate.priceN
existingRate.interval1
existingRate.intervalN
existingRate.gracePeriod
existingRate.activationDate

should all come from Sippy.
