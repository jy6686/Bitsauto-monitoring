One Change I Would Make

Current proposed lifecycle:

pending_rates
↓
awaiting_approval
↓
approved
↓
ready_to_send
↓
activated

I would simplify to:

pending_rates
↓
awaiting_approval
↓
approved
↓
activated

Reason:

ready_to_send has no real business meaning if sending happens immediately after approval.

You already have:

sheet_generated
email_sent

from #328.

Those audit flags tell you whether the send occurred.

Having:

approved
ready_to_send
activated

often creates stuck jobs later.

Unless you plan:

Approved today
Send tomorrow

I would skip ready_to_send for Sprint A.

Approval Controls

I would require:

Submit for Approval

Only if:

Template Linked

Otherwise:

"No template linked to this job."

and disable submission.

Approve

Manager should see:

Client
Product
Template
Destination Count
Notification Type

before approving.

No blind approval.

Reject

Require:

rejection_reason

Mandatory.

Not optional.

Otherwise six months later nobody remembers why it was rejected.

Activation Rule

I strongly support:

    ↓

but add:

activated_at
activated_by

as well.

Not only:

activated_at

You will eventually want to know who activated the client.
