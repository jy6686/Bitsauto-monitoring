Destination Catalog Wiring & Approved Destination Logic

Purpose of Wiring the Destination Catalog

The reason we need to properly wire and implement the Destination Catalog is that we have a very large destination database consisting of tens of thousands of destination codes.

For example:

•⁠  ⁠Approximately 65,000 destination codes may exist within a single product.
•⁠  ⁠When multiplied across multiple products (First Class, Business Class, Special Bravo, Special Charlie, etc.), the total number of destination records can exceed 150,000 entries.

These destinations are uploaded into the system as the master destination repository.

⸻

Approved Destination Workflow

The objective is not to offer all uploaded destinations commercially.

Instead, the workflow should be:

Step 1

Load all destination codes into the Destination Catalog.

Step 2

Review and approve only the destinations that should be commercially available.

Step 3

Approved destinations automatically become available throughout the platform, including:

•⁠  ⁠Rate Manager
•⁠  ⁠Rate Editor
•⁠  ⁠Send Rates
•⁠  ⁠Voice Trading Desk
•⁠  ⁠Product Management
•⁠  ⁠Routing Management

Any destination that is not approved should remain unavailable for commercial use.

⸻

Destination Hierarchy Enforcement

The platform must follow the destination hierarchy.

Example:

Pakistan
└── Mobile
└── Ufone

If only Ufone is approved, then only Ufone should appear in the commercial modules.

If additional operators are approved later, they should automatically become available.

At present, if only:

•⁠  ⁠Pakistan
•⁠  ⁠Pakistan Mobile
•⁠  ⁠Ufone

are visible, while other operators or breakouts are missing, then the hierarchy mapping and wiring need to be reviewed and corrected.

⸻

High-Cost Destination Protection

Some destinations contain extremely expensive breakout codes.

Example:

USA
└── Premium
└── Satellite
└── Inmarsat
└── Iridium
└── Specialized High-Cost Routes

Certain codes may cost:

•⁠  ⁠$2.00
•⁠  ⁠$2.50
•⁠  ⁠$3.00+

per minute.

Offering these codes unintentionally can create significant revenue losses.

Therefore:

Default Rule

High-cost destinations should remain blocked by default.

Approval Rule

Only explicitly approved destinations and breakouts should be made commercially available.

Commercial Rule

If a destination is not approved, it should not:

•⁠  ⁠Appear in Rate Manager
•⁠  ⁠Appear in Send Rates
•⁠  ⁠Appear in Product Pricing
•⁠  ⁠Be included in customer rate sheets

⸻

Destination Availability Logic

The platform should operate according to the following rule:

Approved Destination

Visible and available for:

•⁠  ⁠Pricing
•⁠  ⁠Rate generation
•⁠  ⁠Customer offers
•⁠  ⁠Product assignment

Unapproved Destination

Hidden from commercial users.

Not available for:

•⁠  ⁠Rate generation
•⁠  ⁠Customer pricing
•⁠  ⁠Product creation
•⁠  ⁠Customer offers

⸻

Naming Convention Review

The current destination naming structure should be reviewed and standardized.

The system should maintain consistent naming for:

•⁠  ⁠Country
•⁠  ⁠Fixed
•⁠  ⁠Mobile
•⁠  ⁠Operator
•⁠  ⁠Breakout
•⁠  ⁠Premium Services
•⁠  ⁠Satellite Services

This naming convention should be used consistently across:

•⁠  ⁠Destination Catalog
•⁠  ⁠Rate Sheets
•⁠  ⁠Product Management
•⁠  ⁠Voice Trading Desk
•⁠  ⁠Customer Pricing

⸻

Expected Outcome

The Destination Catalog should become the master destination repository for the entire platform.

All destinations are loaded once, but only approved destinations become commercially available.

This ensures:

•⁠  ⁠Controlled destination offerings.
•⁠  ⁠Protection against high-cost routing losses.
•⁠  ⁠Consistent destination hierarchy.
•⁠  ⁠Proper visibility in Rate Manager and commercial modules.
•⁠  ⁠Standardized destination naming across the platform.
•⁠  ⁠Accurate product and rate management.

Without proper Destination Catalog wiring, approved destinations will not appear correctly in commercial modules, hierarchy relationships will be incomplete, and destination visibility across the platform will remain inconsistent.
Customer Rate Sheet Rules & Format Management Requirements

Overview

The platform should include a comprehensive Rate Sheet Rules Engine similar to advanced carrier trading platforms, where customer rate sheets are generated based on predefined templates, pricing rules, billing increments, and customer-specific preferences.

The objective is to standardize rate sheet generation while allowing flexibility for different customer requirements.

⸻

Customer Rate Sheet Template Management

The system should support configurable rate sheet templates.

Each template should define:

Required Columns

•⁠  ⁠Minimum Duration (Optional)
•⁠  ⁠Product Type
•⁠  ⁠Notes (Optional)

Column Mapping

The platform should automatically generate customer-specific rate sheets using predefined templates and business rules.

The system must support:
