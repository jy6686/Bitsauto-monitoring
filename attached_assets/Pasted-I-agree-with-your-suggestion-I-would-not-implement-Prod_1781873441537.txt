I agree with your suggestion.

I would not implement Product Membership Management using specific client names like:

internal-ptcl
PUSHTOTALK
test202

Those are only useful as temporary test data.

Better Approach

The system should work for:

Existing Accounts
+
Future Accounts

automatically.

Recommended Model

Instead of:

Product
 ↓
Manually Assigned Client Names

Use:

Product
 ↓
Account Attributes
 ↓
Automatic Membership

Examples:

Rule	Product
Account Prefix = 1	First Class
Account Prefix = 2	Business Class
Account Prefix = 6	Special Bravo
Account Prefix = 7	Special Charlie

or

Rule	Product
Tariff Profile	Product
Service Plan	Product
Product Code	Product

depending on how your provisioning works.

Why This Is Better

Today:

100 Accounts

Tomorrow:

1000 Accounts

You don't want somebody manually assigning every account.

When a new account is created via Client Wizard:

Select Product = First Class

System automatically:

Create Account
Create Tariff
Assign Product

No extra step.

Product Should Be a Core Account Property

I would treat Product like:

Account Name
Currency
Billing Type
Product

A fundamental account attribute.

Then Send Rate becomes:

Select Product
↓
Find All Accounts
WHERE product = FC
↓
Resolve Tariffs
↓
Push

No client-name logic.

Membership UI Still Useful

Even with automatic assignment, I would still build:

Product View
FC
 └─ 250 Accounts
Unassigned View
Accounts with NULL product
Conflict View
Invalid assignments

But those become governance tools, not the primary source of truth.

My Recommendation

For long-term scalability:

Product = Account Attribute

not

Product = List of Client Names

That way:

Existing accounts can be classified once.
New accounts automatically inherit product membership during provisioning.
Send Rate, Notifications, Reporting, Invoicing, and Governance all use the same product field.
Adding Product #5 or Product #6 requires no redesign.

That aligns much better with how you've described your operation: products drive commercial behavior, while each account still keeps its own Sippy tariff.
