🧠 What I would implement next (in order of real value)
🥇 1. Execution Reliability Layer (still #1, non-negotiable)

Even though everything “works”, this is the thing that will break you in production.

You still have:

retries missing
infra vs carrier confusion
noisy synthetic failures polluting AI Ops
What I would implement:
retry wrapper for Sippy calls:
max 2 retries
exponential backoff (3s → 6s)
classify failures:
INFRA_FAILURE (timeout, network, XML-RPC error)
CARRIER_FAILURE (SIP response codes, rejection)
exclude INFRA_FAILURE from:
ASR baseline
anomaly triggers
still log INFRA_FAILURE into a separate “health noise stream”

👉 This is your data correctness foundation

Without this, everything else lies sometimes.

🥈 2. “Truth Layer” for AI Ops (not more signals — cleaner signals)

Right now AI Ops is powerful but slightly “loud”.

Next improvement is not adding signals — it’s:

making signals more honest

Add:
signal confidence score (0–1)
synthetic test failure = low confidence
repeated carrier failure = high confidence
source tagging:
synthetic
live_traffic
manual_test
deduplication window:
same carrier + same issue within 15 min = 1 signal

👉 This prevents alert storms

🥉 3. System-wide “Replay Mode” (VERY high leverage)

You already started this idea but didn’t fully formalize it.

You now have:

routing decisions
SIP traces
carrier scoring
synthetic runs

But you still cannot answer:

“what did the system believe at the exact moment of decision?”

Add a replay engine:

For any call:

reconstruct:
routing weights at that time
carrier scores at that time
selected rule path
show “decision snapshot”

This makes your system:

fully explainable per call
