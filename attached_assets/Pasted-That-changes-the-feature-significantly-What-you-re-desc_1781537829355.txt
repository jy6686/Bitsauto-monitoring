That changes the feature significantly.

What you're describing is not a cap rule anymore, because the call is not ending when the cap is reached.

Your desired flow is:

Caller ↔ Vendor RTP Direct

0-30 sec
Normal conversation

30 sec
Start recording vendor audio

60 sec
Governance event fires

Vendor B-leg disconnected
↓
Replay vendor recording
↓
Caller keeps listening
↓
Caller eventually hangs up
Architectural Impact

The A-leg becomes the owner of call termination:

Current Governance:
Cap hit → Cut vendor → End scenario

Your New Scenario:
Cap hit → Cut vendor → Start replay
Replay continues
Caller decides when to disconnect
New Rule Type Recommendation

Instead of:

cap_and_vendor_buffer_replay

I would define:

vendor_buffer_replay

or

vendor_leg_record_and_replay

because:

Call does not terminate at cap.
Cap simply triggers the vendor cut.
A-leg remains active indefinitely.
Required Configuration
Action: vendor_buffer_replay

Direct RTP: Enabled
Record Start: 30 sec
Replay Start: 60 sec
Replay Mode: Loop
Max Replay Length: Unlimited
Caller Controls Disconnect: Yes
Important Risks
1. Infinite Call Duration

If the caller never hangs up:

Vendor cut at 60 sec
↓
Replay loops for 20 minutes
↓
A-leg remains connected

This creates:

Longer channel occupancy
Billing implications
Increased concurrent call counts

I strongly recommend adding:

Maximum Replay Duration

Example:

15 min
30 min
60 min
Unlimited

even if the default is very high.

2. Direct RTP Recording Dependency

You still need:

RTP Forking
or
SIPREC
or
Media Duplication

because pure Direct RTP means:

Caller ↔ Vendor

and Asterisk has no audio to replay later.

This remains the biggest technical dependency.

3. Analytics

The governance platform should separately track:

Vendor Duration
Replay Duration
Total Call Duration

Example:

Vendor Live Time: 60 sec
Replay Time: 8 min
Total Call: 9 min

Otherwise billing and reporting become misleading.

Recommendation

For this use case, I would build:

Action:
vendor_buffer_replay

with:

Direct RTP enabled
Vendor-only audio capture
Vendor B-leg cut at trigger time
Replay starts immediately
Caller controls disconnect
Configurable maximum replay duration
Separate analytics for live vendor time vs replay time

That matches your stated requirement much better than a traditional cap-and-replay rule. The key technical question for the developers is still:

Can the current Asterisk/Sippy environment capture a copy of vendor RTP while Direct RTP is active?

If the answer is no, then RTP forking/SIPREC must be solved first before any replay feature can work.
