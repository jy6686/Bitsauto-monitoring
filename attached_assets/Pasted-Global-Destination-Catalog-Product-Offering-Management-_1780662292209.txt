Global Destination Catalog & Product Offering Management Overview The platform should include a centralized Global Destination Catalog containing all worldwide destinations and their hierarchical destination structure. This module will become the master repository for all destinations used in routing, pricing, rate generation, and customer product offerings. ⸻ Global Destination Hierarchy Each destination should support a multi-level hierarchy. Level 1 – Parent Destination Examples: * Pakistan * India * Bangladesh * United Kingdom * United States These represent country-level destinations. ⸻ Level 2 – Child Destination Examples: Pakistan ├── Fixed ├── Mobile India ├── Fixed ├── Mobile United Kingdom ├── Fixed ├── Mobile This level separates destination categories. ⸻ Level 3 – Breakout Destination Examples: Pakistan Mobile ├── 9230 – Jazz ├── 9231 – Zong ├── 9232 – Warid ├── 9233 – Ufone ├── 9234 – Telenor India Mobile ├── Airtel ├── Jio ├── Vodafone ├── BSNL United Kingdom Mobile ├── Vodafone UK ├── O2 ├── EE ├── Three UK The system should support unlimited breakout levels where required. ⸻ Destination Master Database The platform should maintain: Destination Information * Country Name * Destination Name * Parent Destination * Child Destination * Breakout Destination * Country Code * Dial Prefix * Operator Name * Product Mapping * Status The destination catalog becomes the single source of truth for the entire platform. ⸻ Destination Selection Workspace A dedicated page should display: Left Panel Global Destination Tree Example: Pakistan ├── Fixed ├── Mobile │ ├── Jazz │ ├── Zong │ ├── Ufone │ ├── Telenor India ├── Fixed ├── Mobile United Kingdom ├── Fixed ├── Mobile Users can: * Expand * Collapse * Search * Filter ⸻ Right Panel Approved Commercial Destinations This panel displays destinations approved for commercial use. Example: Approved Destinations ✓ Pakistan Mobile ✓ Pakistan Jazz ✓ Pakistan Zong ✓ India Mobile ✓ Bangladesh Mobile ⸻ Allowed Destination Management The business team should not necessarily offer all A-Z destinations. The system should allow: Available Destinations All global destinations stored in the master database. Allowed Destinations Only destinations approved by management. Blocked Destinations Destinations not available for: * Rate generation * Customer offers * Product creation * Commercial trading Blocked destinations should not appear in pricing workflows. ⸻ Destination Approval Workflow When a new destination is required: 1. User searches destination. 2. Selects destination. 3. Submits approval request. 4. Manager reviews request. 5. Destination becomes commercially available. ⸻ Product & Rate Integration Approved destinations automatically become available in: * Rate Management * Product Management * Voice Trading Desk * Routing Management * Customer Pricing Example: Pakistan Mobile Products: * First Class * Business Class * Special Bravo * Special Charlie Each product can maintain separate rates for the same destination. ⸻ Market Pricing Intelligence For approved destinations, the platform should support: * Current Vendor Cost * Best Available Cost * Previous Cost * Customer Sell Rate * Margin Analysis This allows traders to quickly determine which destinations are commercially active. ⸻ Search & Drill-Down Features Users should be able to: Search By * Country * Destination * Operator * Prefix * Product Drill Down Pakistan → Mobile → Jazz → First Class → Customer Rates → Vendor Costs ⸻ Benefits This module creates a centralized destination repository where: * All global destinations are maintained. * Only approved destinations can be offered commercially. * Product-based pricing is controlled. * Destination management is standardized. * Traders can easily find, approve, and offer destinations. * Rate management becomes consistent across the entire platform. The Global Destination Catalog will become the foundation for Routing, Pricing, Voice Trading, Product Management, and Customer Rate Distribution
