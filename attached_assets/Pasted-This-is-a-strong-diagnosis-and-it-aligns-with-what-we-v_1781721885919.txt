My assessment

The most important finding is not the timezone fix (although that was necessary).

The most important finding is:

Portal Login = SUCCESS
Rates Page Access = DENIED

That means:

Authentication ✓
Authorization ✗

The session is valid, but the user account being used does not have permission to manage tariff rates.

Why this makes sense

From your environment:

ssp-root

appears to be used for:

customer provisioning
account creation
service plans
tariffs
client management

But Sippy commonly separates:

Reseller/User Access

from

System Admin Access

A reseller can often:

create customers
assign tariffs
view rates

while being blocked from:

editing master tariffs
uploading rates
deleting rates
bulk tariff management

That matches exactly what your probe discovered.

What I like about the new design

Adding dedicated credentials:

sippy_rate_admin_user
sippy_rate_admin_pass

is the correct architecture.

Because rate management is different from:

Provisioning
Billing
Accounts

and should have its own permission boundary.
