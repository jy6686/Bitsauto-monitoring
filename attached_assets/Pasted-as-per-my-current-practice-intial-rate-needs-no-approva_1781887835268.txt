I agree with you for the telecom wholesale business you operate.

The approval model that works in banks, procurement systems, or enterprise software often becomes harmful in carrier trading because speed is part of the business value.

What actually happens in wholesale telecom

Vendor sends rate update:

Vendor sends rate sheet
      ↓
You analyze impact
      ↓
You decide to send client rates
      ↓
Client receives rates
      ↓
Client routing team evaluates
      ↓
Traffic moves

If you insert:

Vendor sends rate sheet
      ↓
KAM prepares rates
      ↓
Approval Queue
      ↓
Manager unavailable
      ↓
Approval delayed
      ↓
Client receives rates 6-12 hours later

then:

Competitor already sent rates
      ↓
Client already selected route
      ↓
Opportunity lost

This is very common in wholesale voice.

My recommendation
Remove approval from operational rate sending

Rate Notifications:

Create Sheet
      ↓
Send Sheet
      ↓
Audit Log
      ↓
Complete

No approval.

No waiting.

No manager bottleneck.

Keep governance, remove approval

Instead of:

Approval Before Send

use:

Audit After Send

Example:

Rate Notification Sent
    By: Junaid
    Time: 14:35 UTC
    Product: FC
    Destinations: 120
    Clients: 87

Management can review later.

The action is recorded but not blocked.

Where approval actually makes sense

I would only use approval for:

1. Product Catalogue Changes
Pakistan Jazz
0.025 → 0.040

This affects everyone.

Approval can be justified.

2. Client Specific Override
Standard FC rate:
0.025

Special client:
0.020

This changes margin.

Approval may be required.

3. Financial Actions
Credit Limit Increase
Payment Release
Large Refund

Approval makes sense.
