The corrections are heading in the right direction, but I'd make a few architectural adjustments before considering it complete.

What I Agree With
Separate SBC Stages

Legacy behavior clearly appears to be:

Email Sent
↓
SBC Mapping Available
↓
SBC Updated
↓
Updated Tariff
↓
Approval Required / Violated Rules
↓
Successful

So splitting:

sbcMappingOk
sbcUpdated

is correct.

Notification Sheet ≠ Tariff

This is probably the most important clarification.

Many telecom operators misunderstand this.

The notification sheet should represent:

Changed Prefixes Only

not:

Complete Tariff Replacement

Meaning:

9230  Jazz
9715  UAE Mobile
4477  UK Mobile

are updated.

Everything else remains untouched.

That matches how Sippy tariffs actually behave.

One Architectural Concern

The status sequence should not be strictly linear.

Today you have:

1
2
3
4
5
6
7

But in reality:

Email Sent
Tariff Updated

can succeed while:

Approval Required

is still pending.

Or:

Tariff Updated

may complete before:

SBC Updated

depending on workflow.

I would store them as independent state flags rather than a hard pipeline.

Example:

emailSent = true
sbcMappingOk = true
sbcUpdated = false
tariffUpdated = true
approvalRequired = true

Then render the timeline dynamically.

Bigger Missing Piece

The job currently tracks:

Email
SBC
Tariff

But it doesn't yet track:

Rate Notification Template Version

Example:

Template ID: 102
Version: 4
Generated: 2026-06-19

Without this, six months later you cannot prove exactly what was sent to the customer.

For auditability I'd add:

template_id
template_version
generated_attachment_hash

to the job record.

Another Missing Stage

Legacy systems usually distinguish:

Generated

from

Sent

Example:

Notification Sheet Generated
↓
Email Sent

Those are not the same event.

You can generate successfully and fail SMTP.

So I'd consider:

Sheet Generated
Email Sent

as separate audit checkpoints.

Client Wizard Integration

The biggest thing from the previous discussion remains:

When a new client is created:

Create Account
Create Tariff
Create Service Plan

should automatically create:

Initial Client Rate Job

Status:

Pending Initial Rates

That becomes the entry point into this entire workflow.

Client Wizard
    ↓
Account
    ↓
Tariff
    ↓

    ↓

    ↓
Notification Sheet Generated
    ↓

    ↓
SBC Mapping Available
    ↓
SBC Updated
    ↓

    ↓

    ↓

And keep the existing Sippy push engine completely untouched.

The strongest part of your current direction is that you're separating:

Commercial Notification Workflow

from

Sippy Rate Update Engine

while allowing them to participate in the same job record. That's much closer to the legacy BitsAuto behavior than treating Send Rate as just another tariff update screen.
