The document correctly identifies that timestamp rendering is inconsistent across the platform. That's a real issue worth fixing.

However, the claim that the export restriction requires timezone standardization first is not accurate:

The 22:00–06:00 restriction is new Date().getUTCHours() — two lines. It is inherently timezone-agnostic because it operates on UTC directly. It does not inherit browser local time, it does not read settings.timezone, it does not depend on any formatter. You can implement it today without touching anything else.

What the global timezone system actually involves

This is not a small add. It is a cross-cutting refactor:

Item	Effort
Add timezone to settings schema + DB	Low
Create formatInPlatformTimezone() utility	Low
Find and replace every toLocaleString(), toString(), raw date render across the whole app	High — this is in reports, CDR tables, NOC, alerts, billing, exports, portals, audit logs
Verify Sippy timestamps are UTC (they are — confirmed by the XML-RPC spec)	Done — no work needed
Test that nothing regresses	Medium
The formatter utility itself is ~20 lines. The find-and-replace pass across the codebase is the real work and it touches many unrelated pages.
