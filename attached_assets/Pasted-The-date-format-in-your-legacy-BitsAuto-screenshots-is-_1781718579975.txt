Both fixes are deployed. Here's a summary of what changed and why:

Bug: Date timezone shift

When you type 2026-06-17 16:30 in the "Active From" field, the old code ran:

new Date("2026-06-17T16:30")  // treats as local time (PKT = UTC+5)
.toISOString()                 // converts to UTC: "2026-06-17T11:30:00Z"
→ Sippy receives "2026-06-17 11:30:00"  ← 5 hours wrong

The legacy BitsAuto system (which successfully changed rates in your screenshots) sends exactly 2026-06-17 13:30 — no conversion, no seconds. That's now what we send.

Fix:
Both date normalizers now do pure string manipulation:

"2026-06-17T16:30" → "2026-06-17 16:30" ✓ (just replace T with space)
"2026-06-17T16:30:00" → "2026-06-17 16:30" ✓ (strip seconds too)
"2026-06-17" → "2026-06-17 00:00" ✓ (date-only gets midnight)
This applies to both the XML-RPC activation_date params and the portal CSV ActivationDate column.
