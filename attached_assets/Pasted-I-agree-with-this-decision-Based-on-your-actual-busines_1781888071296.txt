Based on your actual business model, I would make one adjustment to the governance architecture before moving forward with #339:

Change the Commercial Default

Current value:

Commercial.Require Approval For Initial Rates = TRUE

Recommended:

Commercial.Require Approval For Initial Rates = FALSE

Reason:

When a client reaches the Initial Rate stage:

Client Wizard
   ↓
Product Selected
   ↓
Tariff Created
   ↓
Service Plan Created
   ↓
Initial Rate Job Created

The commercial approval has effectively already happened during onboarding.

The KAM, Product, and Commercial teams already agreed:

Client creation
Product assignment
Tariff creation
Service plan creation

Adding another approval before sending the first rate sheet creates duplication, not governance.

Suggested Initial Rate Lifecycle

Instead of:

Pending Rates
    ↓
Awaiting Approval
    ↓
Approved
    ↓
Activated

Use:

Pending Rates
    ↓
Template Created
    ↓
Rate Sheet Sent
    ↓
Activated

Keep full audit:

created_by
created_at

template_created_by
template_created_at

sent_by
sent_at

activated_by
activated_at

This gives accountability without delay.

Where Approval SHOULD Remain

I would keep approval capability available in the governance framework but only consume it for high-risk actions:
