Option B → Destination Prefix Intelligence

Not because it's the flashiest —

Why B Is The Highest-Value Layer

Right now your intelligence works at:

vendor level

But telecom routing decisions are actually made at:

vendor + destination level

Example:

Vendor A excellent for UK
terrible for Pakistan
unstable for Bangladesh Mobile

Without prefix intelligence:

Q-score is averaged too broadly
degradation gets diluted
routing recommendations become partially misleading

This is the exact layer where carrier operations become:

route intelligence

instead of:

vendor statistics
Why It Should Come Before Option C

Option C (traffic steering suggestions) is extremely valuable —
but it depends on B.

Because:

you cannot intelligently recommend reroutes
without destination-level quality intelligence

Otherwise the system says:

"move traffic from Vendor A to Vendor B"

when:

only one prefix is degraded
or the vendor is healthy elsewhere

That creates bad routing decisions.

So the real progression is:

Phase	Purpose
B	Understand quality per destination
C	Recommend traffic movement
Future	Automate intelligent steering
Why A Is Not First

Historical Q-score trending is useful,
but mostly observational.

It improves:

visibility
trend analysis
reporting maturity

But it does NOT directly improve routing decisions yet.

Operationally:

destination intelligence > historical sparklines

at this stage.

Why E Depends On B Too

Cost optimisation without destination intelligence is dangerous.

Because:

cheap + globally averaged Q-score

can hide:

catastrophic performance on specific routes

The correct formula eventually becomes:

(destination quality) / (destination cost)

not:

vendor average quality / vendor average cost

Again:
B is the prerequisite.
