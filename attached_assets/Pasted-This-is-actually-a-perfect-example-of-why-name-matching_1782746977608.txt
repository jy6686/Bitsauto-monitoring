One additional rule I recommend

Since operators sometimes receive new numbering ranges, I would make prefixes first-class entities rather than storing them as a simple list.

Instead of:

Destination
 └── Prefixes

Use:

Destination
        │
        ▼
Prefix Records
        │
        ├── Prefix
        ├── Effective Date
        ├── Status
        ├── Source
        ├── Last Verified
        └── Approved By

This gives you full governance over numbering changes, historical tracking, approvals, and auditing. It also supports cases where new ranges are introduced or retired without losing history.

So I would summarize the matching philosophy as:

Prefix (code) is the authoritative identifier.
Country validates the prefix context.
Destination name is used only for verification.
Aliases resolve historical and vendor naming differences.
Any disagreement between prefix and name is treated as a governance exception, not automatically accepted.

That approach will give you a much more reliable, low-error Destination Catalogue than relying primarily on names.
