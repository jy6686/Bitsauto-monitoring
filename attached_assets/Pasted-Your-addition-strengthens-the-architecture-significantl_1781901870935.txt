Better Validation Rule Structure

Instead of:

selected_action

I would eventually move to:

selected_action
approval_required

Example:

{
  "rule": "suspect_increase",
  "action": "ignore",
  "approvalRequired": true
}

or

{
  "rule": "suspect_increase",
  "action": "reject_country",
  "approvalRequired": false
}
Why This Fits Your Business Better

Because your earlier decision was:

Initial Rates
NO approval

But later you may want:

Exceptional pricing
Approval

or

Rate decrease > 50%
Approval

or

Margin below floor
Approval

without changing the actual validation action.

Recommended Future Matrix
Rule	Action	Approval
Suspect Increase	Ignore	On/Off
Suspect Decrease	Reject Country	On/Off
Effective Date Greater Than Limit	Reject Country	On/Off
Effective Date Older Than Limit	Ignore	On/Off
Pending Increase Exceeded	Ignore	On/Off

This gives Commercial complete control without requiring developers to change logic every time a policy changes.

For your platform, I think Action + Approval Toggle is a better long-term design than treating "Approval Required" as an action itself.
