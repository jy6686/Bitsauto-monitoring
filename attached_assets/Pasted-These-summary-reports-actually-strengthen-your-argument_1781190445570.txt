What this proves

The billing engine is not simply rating:

923069306001

It is rating:

1923069306001
2923069306001
6923069306001
7923069306001

or other product-encoded prefixes.

Therefore:

last 10 digits

may be acceptable for finding candidates,

but it is not acceptable as the final financial identity.

Why this matters for invoices

If two products share:

3069306001

but have different sell rates:

FC = 0.0230
BC = 0.0194
SC = 0.0400

then a wrong match could:

Overcharge customer
Undercharge customer
Show wrong margin
Produce wrong invoice
Create disputes

Exactly the concern you've been raising.

What I would do before Trust Center

I would move these to the top of the commercial roadmap:

Product Registry completion
Rate Repository
Rate Manager
Rate Integrity Audit
Product-aware P&L matching
Invoice validation

Only after those are proven would I trust automated invoicing.

Most important technical change

For every governed call, store:

Product Code
Original CLD
Normalized CLD
Prefix

Example:

Product = FC
Original CLD = 1923069306001
Destination = 923069306001

Then Billing Check matches:

FC + 923069306001 + time

instead of:

3069306001 + time

That removes the cross-product ambiguity entirely.
