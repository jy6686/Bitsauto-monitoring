I agree with about 80–90% of this architecture, but I would make one important correction.

Product Registry should not be the origin of product_code

The document says:

0.2 Commercial Identity Layer
0.3 Product Registry Authority
↓
Asterisk stamps product_code

I would reverse part of that thinking.

Product Registry = Source of Definition

Product Registry defines:

FC
BC
SB
SC

Pricing Template
Routing Template
Tariff Mapping
Prefix Mapping

That's correct.

But Product Code Origin = Call Origination Decision

The real question is:

At the moment the call enters Asterisk, how do we know it's FC or BC?

Possible sources:

Source	Reliability
Dialed Prefix (1/2/6/7)	Very High
Customer Assigned Product	High
Routing Group	High
Product Registry	Definition only
Post-call CLD analysis	Low

If the customer already dials:

1923...
2923...
6923...
7923...

then the product identity already exists before Product Registry lookup.

Asterisk can immediately stamp:

BITSAUTO_PRODUCT=FC
BITSAUTO_PRODUCT=BC

based on the incoming prefix.

Then Product Registry validates and enriches it.

What I think the real foundation is

Not:

Commercial Identity Layer
↓
Product Registry

Instead:

Commercial Identity Layer
├─ Product Code
├─ Original CLD
├─ Customer
├─ Account
├─ Call UUID
└─ Prefix

Then:

Product Registry
↓
Rate Repository
↓
Tariff Mapping

consume that identity.

The bigger architectural win

I think you've discovered something even more important:

Today BitsAuto has:

Customer Identity
Vendor Identity
Route Identity
Deal Identity

But it lacks:

Call Identity

A true telecom operating system needs:

Commercial Call Identity (CCI)

Every call becomes an object.

Example:

CCI-20260611-123456

Customer: ABC Telecom
Product: FC
Original CLD: 1923069306001
Destination: Pakistan Jazz
Tariff: FC-PK-JAZZ
Vendor: PTCL
Revenue: 0.0067
Cost: 0.0001
Margin: 0.0066

Then:

Billing Verification reads CCI
Deal Workspace reads CCI
Finance reads CCI
Revenue Protection reads CCI
Future AI reads CCI

Nobody re-matches anything.

My recommendation

Before building Trust Center, QA, Route Certification, etc., I would create a dedicated roadmap item:

Priority 0.2
Commercial Call Identity (CCI)

Deliverables:

call_uuid
customer_id
account_id
product_code
product_prefix
original_cld
normalized_cld
tariff_id
vendor_id
start_time

Persisted at call creation.

Priority 0.3
Product Registry Authority
Priority 0.4
Rate Repository
Priority 0.5
Unified Reconciliation Engine

Customer CDR + Vendor CDR + P&L attached to CCI.

Priority 0.6
Rate Integrity Audit
Priority 0.7
Invoice Validation

Only after 0.7 would I trust customer invoices.

One final observation from all the reports you've shared:

The biggest risk is not actually the missing leading 1.

The biggest risk is:

Product Identity exists in Sippy
but does not exist as a first-class object in BitsAuto.

That's the architectural gap causing Billing Verification, Margin Truth, Vendor Cost, P&L, and Invoice concerns to all surface at the same time. Once CCI exists, those problems become one problem solved once rather than six problems solved separately.
