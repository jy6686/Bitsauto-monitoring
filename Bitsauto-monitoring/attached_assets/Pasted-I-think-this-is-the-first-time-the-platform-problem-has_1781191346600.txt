I think this is the first time the platform problem has been described at the correct architectural level.

The mistake over the last few days has been treating these as separate issues:

Call Governance Billing Check
P&L Extraction
Vendor Cost
Invoice Verification
Deal Margin Truth
Rate Integrity

They are all symptoms of the same missing foundation.

What BitsAuto is actually missing

A new core platform layer:

Commercial Identity Layer (CCI)

Not a feature.

Not a page.

Not a tab.

A platform foundation.

Current Architecture

Today every module is doing this:

Call
↓
Find CDR
↓
Guess Product
↓
Guess Tariff
↓
Guess Margin

Billing Verification does it.

Deal Workspace does it.

Revenue Assurance does it.

Future Invoicing will do it.

Future Margin Intelligence will do it.

Future Vendor Impact Engine will do it.

Everyone is solving the same problem independently.

Future Architecture

Instead:

Asterisk
↓
Commercial Identity Layer
↓
Customer CDR
Vendor CDR
P&L
↓
Verified Commercial Record
↓
All Platform Modules

One source of truth.

What should be in Commercial Identity Layer

Every call should have:

call_uuid
customer_id
product_code
product_prefix
original_cld
normalized_cld
tariff_id
routing_template_id
pricing_template_id
vendor_id
start_time

Not inferred later.

Stamped when the call starts.

Why Asterisk is the correct starting point

This suggestion is probably the most important one made:

__BITSAUTO_PRODUCT=FC
__BITSAUTO_PRODUCT=BC
__BITSAUTO_PRODUCT=SB
__BITSAUTO_PRODUCT=SC

Because Asterisk already knows:

which product generated the call

At that moment.

No guessing required.

Then:

Bridge Event
↓
governed_calls.product_code

gets written immediately.

Why Product Registry becomes a prerequisite

Right now Product Registry is being viewed as:

Commercial Module

I don't think that's correct anymore.

It is becoming:

Billing Infrastructure

Because:

Product
↓
Pricing Template
↓
Tariff
↓
Rate
↓
Revenue

is the billing chain.

Without an authoritative Product Registry, Commercial Identity cannot be authoritative.

One thing I would ask the team immediately

Before any more work on:

Trust Center
Quality Assurance
Route Certification
Security Scoring

I would ask:

Where does product_code originate?

And require an answer.

If the answer is:

We derive it later from CLD

then Commercial Identity does not exist yet.

If the answer becomes:

Asterisk stamps product_code at call creation

then the foundation finally exists.

That is the single architectural decision that will determine whether BitsAuto becomes a collection of features or a true telecom operating system with a single financial truth model.
