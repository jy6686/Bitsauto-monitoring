I would tackle CDR disposition fidelity first.

Reason:
everything after it depends on the quality and granularity of the underlying CDR outcome data.

Right now the roadmap branches at that gate:

Feature	Depends on disposition fidelity?
NER feasibility	✅
ACD/FAS overlays	partially
RCA summaries	strongly
Incident enrichment	strongly

If the CDR feed does not reliably expose:

SIP response codes
disposition states
busy/no-answer/reject
cause codes
early-media indicators

then:

NER becomes weak or misleading
RCA confidence becomes unreliable
ACD/FAS overlays lose diagnostic precision

So this is the correct foundational investigation.

What I Would Verify
1. Actual CDR fields present

Specifically inspect live cached records for:

disconnect_reason
sip_code
hangup_cause
disposition
answered
duration
billsec
release_source
q850
cause
disconnect_side

and any Sippy-specific equivalents.

2. Fidelity of outcomes

Determine whether the system can distinguish:

Outcome	Needed for
ANSWERED	ASR
BUSY	NER
NOANSWER	NER
REJECTED	RCA
NO ROUTE	RCA
EARLY MEDIA	FAS
NORMAL CLEARING	analytics
NETWORK FAILURE	RCA

This is the critical gate.

3. Statistical usefulness

Even if fields exist:

are they populated consistently?
do vendors normalize them?
are codes stable across carriers?
are values trustworthy enough operationally?

Carrier-grade systems often fail here because:

vendors emit inconsistent cause semantics

That must be understood before building analytics on top.

Why This Is The Correct Next Move

This is:

read-only
low-risk
architectural due diligence
no telemetry changes
no schema churn

Exactly the right kind of next step after stabilization.

Then The Correct Sequence

After disposition validation:

If fidelity is GOOD:
NER
ACD/FAS overlays
RCA summaries
If fidelity is PARTIAL:

skip NER temporarily and move to:

ACD/FAS overlays
RCA using available signals
later ingestion enrichment

That decision should be data-driven, not aspirational.

My Recommendation

Start with:

live CDR disposition field inspection

because it determines the viability and trustworthiness of the next entire operational intelligence layer.
