In telecommunications, ASR and NER are two essential Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) tracked via Call Detail Records (CDRs) to measure network performance. ASR measures overall call completion, while NER evaluates the network's technical ability to deliver a call without being skewed by end-user behavior.The formulas, calculations, and distinctions for both are detailed below.1. Answer Seizure Ratio (ASR)ASR determines the percentage of attempted calls that are successfully answered by the receiving party. It provides immediate insight into network health, though it can be heavily influenced by end-user behavior (e.g., people rejecting calls or getting busy signals).Formula:\(ASR(\%)=\left(\frac{\text{Number\ of\ Answered\ Calls}}{\text{Total\ Call\ Attempts}}\right)\times 100\)Total Call Attempts (Seizures): Every time the network initiates the signaling process to route a call.Answered Calls: Calls that successfully reach the destination and result in an "answer" signal from the far-end terminal.What it indicates: A low ASR can flag technical issues like SIP signaling failures and network misconfigurations, but it can also simply mean customers aren't answering.2. Network Effectiveness Ratio (NER)NER isolates pure network infrastructure performance by removing end-user behavior (such as busy signals, not answering, or rejecting calls) from the equation. It is defined by the International Telecommunication Union (ITU) as a more accurate measure of internal interconnect and route quality.Formula:\(NER(\%)=\left(\frac{\text{Answered}+\text{User\ Busy}+\text{Ring\ No\ Answer}+\text{Terminal\ Rejects}}{\text{Total\ Call\ Attempts}}\right)\times 100\)Answered: Call reached the end-user and was picked up.User Busy: The recipient was on another call (network technically delivered the call, so it counts as a success).Ring No Answer: The phone rang but the user did not answer.Terminal Rejects: The destination device was unavailable.What it indicates: A high NER combined with a low ASR means the network is functioning perfectly, but your called contacts are simply not picking up.Industry BenchmarksExcellent ASR: >60%Acceptable ASR: 40% – 50%Ideal NER: Typically expected to be higher than ASR (often aiming for >90-95% depending on carrier agreements).You can read more about how these calculations are structured in the ITU-T Rec.
