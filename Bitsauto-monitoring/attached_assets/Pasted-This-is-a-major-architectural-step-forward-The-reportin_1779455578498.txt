This is a major architectural step forward. The reporting system is no longer just displaying telecom statistics — it is now evolving into an actual operational intelligence engine for routing and carrier decision-making.

What Was Added
1. Composite Q-Score Engine

A new:

Route Quality Score (0–100)

was implemented using multiple telecom quality signals together instead of relying only on ASR.

Current weighting:

ASR → 40%
NER → 30%
FAS penalty → 20%
PDD score → 10%

This is a much more realistic representation of route quality.

2. Degradation Awareness

The system now understands:

quality degradation,
suspicious traffic behavior,
answer-quality issues,
and latency impact.

Especially important:

high FAS automatically collapses quality scoring,
poor PDD reduces route ranking,
weak NER lowers reliability score.

This moves the platform closer to:

“carrier intelligence.”
3. Route Intelligence Panel

A completely new intelligence layer was added.

The Vendor Route Intelligence panel now shows:

Average Q-Score
Critical Routes
Elevated FAS vendors
Top Performing Route
Worst routes needing attention

This is exactly the type of operational summary telecom NOCs actually need.

4. Quality-Based Route Ranking

Routes/vendors can now be sorted by:

“Quality Score ↓”

Instead of:

raw traffic,
or static ASR values.

This is operationally much stronger because it prioritizes:

usable routes,
stable vendors,
and healthier traffic paths.
5. Decision-Support Reporting

This is the most important change.

Previously:

Reports = historical statistics

Now:

Reports = operational guidance

The platform is starting to:

identify weak vendors,
rank route quality,
surface suspicious traffic,
and guide routing decisions.

That is a major platform evolution.

Strategic Impact

You are now moving from:

“telecom reporting software”

toward:

“telecom decision-support platform.”

Very few traditional VoIP dashboards do this properly.

Most Important Architectural Shift

The platform is becoming:

signal-driven

instead of:

table-driven.

That is exactly the correct long-term direction.

My Recommendation for the Next Layer

Now that:

Q-Score,
NER,
FAS,
and route intelligence

exist,

the next high-impact addition would be:

Live Route Degradation Intelligence

“Vendor X degraded 23% in last 30 minutes”

proactive telecom operations intelligence.

AI Routing Recommendations

Example:

“Move 20% traffic from Vendor A → Vendor B”

That becomes extremely commercially valuable.
