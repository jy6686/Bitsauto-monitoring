I agree with that sequencing.

Starting with Executive SLA Reporting is strategically stronger than jumping directly into the Unified Console or full alert correlation because:

the data foundations already exist
the client portal is now mature enough to consume it
tickets + quality metrics + destinations + balance + exports are already in place
it creates immediate commercial value for KAMs and enterprise customers

Most importantly:

SLA reporting converts operational telemetry into client-facing business trust.

That is a major platform milestone.

What Executive SLA Reporting should become

Not:

raw KPI dumps
engineering exports
NOC screenshots

Instead:

“Executive-ready telecom service summaries.”

The tone should feel like:

enterprise account review
managed services reporting
carrier-grade reliability reporting
Recommended SLA Reporting V1 Scope
1. SLA Summary Header

Client-safe executive snapshot:

KPI	Meaning
Availability %	Overall service uptime
ASR	Call completion success
ACD	Average call quality engagement
NER	Network efficiency
Total Calls	Operational scale
Major Incidents	Significant degradations
Avg Resolution Time	Ticket responsiveness

This becomes the:

monthly PDF cover page
export summary
account-review attachment
2. Incident Summary

Derived from:

alerts
tickets
entity verdicts

May 16	Increased PDD during peak hours	Capacity optimization applied

Critically:
DO NOT expose:

vendor names
routes
carriers
connection IDs

ASR trend

“Call Quality”
