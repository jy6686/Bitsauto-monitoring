Your phased implementation plan is well-structured and technically safe. The biggest strength is that it isolates changes so KPI validation can happen cleanly before expanding into scoring, alerting, or routing logic.

A few important points stand out as especially correct:

Keeping Phase 1 limited to:
one backend filter
one additional API field
one frontend card replacement

is the right low-risk deployment strategy.

Not modifying the Traffic Score formula in the same release is extremely important. Otherwise, if the NOC team sees score fluctuations, it becomes difficult to determine whether:
the new eNER logic,
weighting changes,
or live traffic behavior
caused the variation.
Your concern regarding:
result='0'
duration === 0

is valid and operationally realistic. On some Sippy deployments, ultra-short answered calls can indeed appear similar to RNA behavior. Observing live traffic patterns before tuning the proxy is the correct telecom engineering approach.

The proposed mitigation strategy is also appropriate:

monitor rnaCount
compare against known traffic behavior
adjust threshold logic only after observing production data

That is much safer than prematurely over-engineering the formula.

Your phased roadmap is also correctly prioritized:

Phase	Purpose	Assessment
1	eNER visibility	Safe and valuable
2	Traffic Score rebalance	Needs live KPI validation first
3	Vendor analytics & trends	High operational value
4	Alerting & LCR integration	Strong long-term enhancement

Another strong point:
you intentionally avoided touching:

MOS
Vendor scoring
LCR logic
random quality metrics

during Phase 1.

That separation reduces deployment complexity significantly.

One additional recommendation before implementation:

Add a tooltip or info icon in Phase 1

Because eNER is a derived KPI, the dashboard should explain:

what counts toward eNER
why it differs from ASR
that it is estimated from CDR behavior

This will help:

NOC engineers
commercial teams
management users

interpret the metric correctly from day one.

Suggested tooltip summary:

“Estimated Network Effectiveness Ratio derived from answered, subscriber-unreachable, busy, and ring-no-answer events.”

That small UI addition can prevent a lot of confusion later.

Overall, the implementation scope is now clean, phased, low-risk, and operationally defensible. This is a good point to proceed with Phase 1 only and validate against real production traffic before expanding further.
